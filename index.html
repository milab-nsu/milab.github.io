Machine Intelligence Lab (MILab) at North South University is a cutting-edge research lab dedicated to advancing the fields of machine learning, computer vision, and natural language processing. Our mission is to drive innovation by developing intelligent algorithms, state-of-the-art models, and real-world applications that push the boundaries of AI research. Through interdisciplinary collaboration, we aim to solve complex problems and publish impactful research that benefits both academia and industry.

Ongoing Projects

  1. Multilingual Financial Large Language Models

    As the global financial industry continues to grow, the need for models that can efficiently process and analyze multilingual financial data becomes increasingly important. This project focuses on building a Multilingual Financial Large Language Model (LLM) that is optimized at the architectural level for financial applications across multiple languages. By re-engineering the model's internal structure and integrating parameter-efficient fine-tuning techniques, we aim to enhance the model’s capability to handle complex financial texts, from regulatory reports to market analysis, in languages such as English, French, and German. This involves adapting the model's layers to efficiently process nuanced financial terms and idioms across different languages while maintaining performance. Our work emphasizes reducing computational costs and improving inference speed, making the model more accessible for real-world financial systems. Additionally, we explore multilingual pretraining strategies and domain-specific fine-tuning to ensure that the LLM aligns with various financial regulatory and market structures worldwide.

  2. Financial Sentiment Analysis Utilizing Large Language Model

    Understanding market sentiment is pivotal in making informed financial decisions, as it directly influences stock prices, investment opportunities, and market trends. This project leverages the power of Large Language Models (LLMs) to analyze financial sentiment from various sources such as news articles, social media, and financial reports. By fine-tuning LLMs on financial-specific data, we aim to build a robust sentiment analysis system that can detect subtle emotional cues, trends, and biases in financial communications. The project explores techniques like prompt engineering and parameter-efficient tuning to adapt the LLM to financial language and jargon, providing investors with accurate and actionable insights. Moreover, we integrate explainable AI methods, ensuring that the sentiment classifications and predictions are transparent and understandable for users. This model will help financial institutions assess market sentiment, enabling better risk management and investment strategies.

  3. Time-series Forecasting

    Time-series forecasting is essential for analyzing and predicting trends in sequential data across various domains, including finance, healthcare, energy, and supply chain management. This project focuses on Time Series Analysis, aiming to model temporal dependencies and uncover patterns in evolving data. By applying machine learning algorithms and advanced statistical models, we seek to improve forecasting accuracy and enable data-driven decision-making. The project explores autoregressive models, recurrent neural networks (RNNs), and Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) architectures, fine-tuned to handle the unique challenges of time series data, such as non-stationarity, seasonality, and volatility. Additionally, we incorporate anomaly detection and feature engineering to enhance predictive performance. This analysis is valuable for industries looking to optimize operations, mitigate risks, and make informed strategic decisions based on future trends.

  4. Vision-Based Body Shape Measurement

    Vision-Based Body Shape Measurement is an innovative approach that utilizes computer vision and image processing techniques to analyze and assess the shape and dimensions of the human body. By leveraging visual data captured from cameras or imaging devices, this method enables accurate measurements of body features without the need for physical interaction or specialized equipment. It has a wide range of applications, from fashion and apparel design to health and fitness, offering a non-invasive and efficient way to obtain detailed body measurements. As technology advances, this technique continues to evolve, promising improved precision and usability in diverse industries.

  5. Improved Fine-tuning of Language Models with Reasoning

    Improved Fine-tuning of Language Models with Reasoning focuses on enhancing the performance of language models by incorporating advanced reasoning techniques during the fine-tuning process. By integrating reasoning capabilities, such as logical thinking and decision-making, into the training pipeline, this approach aims to equip models with a deeper understanding of complex tasks. This method seeks to refine the models' ability to process and generate more contextually relevant, accurate, and coherent responses, particularly in challenging scenarios that require multi-step problem-solving. As language models become more sophisticated, improved fine-tuning with reasoning promises to push the boundaries of natural language understanding and generation.

  6. Information Retrieval from Legal Documents

    Information Retrieval from Legal Documents involves the process of extracting relevant data and insights from complex legal texts using advanced search and analysis techniques. This approach leverages natural language processing and machine learning tools to navigate large volumes of legal information, such as statutes, case law, and contracts, in order to efficiently identify pertinent details. The goal is to enhance accessibility, improve legal research workflows, and support informed decision-making. As legal documents can be dense and highly technical, advancements in information retrieval aim to make this critical data more accessible and actionable for professionals in the legal domain.

  7. Waste Detection

    Waste Detection involves the use of advanced technologies to identify and monitor waste materials in various environments, such as urban areas, industrial sites, or natural ecosystems. This approach combines sensors, computer vision, and data analytics to detect, classify, and track waste, enabling efficient management and timely intervention. The goal is to address environmental challenges by automating waste identification, improving waste management systems, and promoting sustainability. As technology evolves, waste detection systems are becoming increasingly accurate and accessible, helping industries, municipalities, and communities to reduce waste-related impact and enhance resource efficiency.
